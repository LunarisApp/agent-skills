# Governance and risk interviews

## Governance sequence

1. Document control: approval mechanism, ownership, review triggers, retirement, external evidence storage.
2. Scope: legal entity, services, people, locations, environments, repositories, suppliers, exclusions, interfaces.
3. Context: strategy, maturity, threats, legal markets, interested parties, contractual needs, climate relevance.
4. Roles: authority, competence, founder constraints, emergency continuity, internal-audit independence.
5. System description: commitments, components, data, people, procedures, subservice organizations, boundaries.
6. Obligations: jurisdictions, privacy roles, contracts, intellectual property, records, notification duties.

## Inventories before risk

Review assets, data processing, sources, services, privileged access, business systems, recovery dependencies, and public claims before selecting risk method.

## Risk method decisions

User defines likelihood/impact scales, scoring method, acceptance threshold, owners, assessment cadence, and treatment authority. Explain choices; never select numeric thresholds or intervals for user.

Construct each risk as asset or process, event/threat/vulnerability, confidentiality/integrity/availability impact, existing controls, inherent assessment, treatment, residual assessment, owner, and acceptance. Do not treat a documentation gap alone as a security risk unless it creates an uncertain event affecting objectives.

## Objectives

Derive objectives from context and risk after treatment decisions. Each objective needs owner, measure, source, baseline when available, target selected by user, review mechanism, and action when missed.
