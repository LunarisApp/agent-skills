# Assurance and framework mapping

## Licensed-source boundary

Public templates contain neither normative requirements nor complete control inventories. Organization supplies an identifier-only local profile only after confirming its licence permits intended digital and AI-assisted use. Use organization-authorized [ISO/IEC 27001:2022 with Amendment 1:2024](https://www.iso.org/standard/88435.html) and [AICPA Trust Services Criteria](https://www.aicpa-cima.com/resources/landing/licensing-for-teams) sources for final interpretation. Do not paste licensed text into repository or prompts unless applicable licence expressly permits it. Require qualified auditor validation before certification or attestation.

## Statement of Applicability

Using authorized profile, review every applicable Annex A identifier required by chosen edition. For each, record applicability, risk-based justification, implementation status, owner, policy/procedure, and expected evidence. Justify exclusions. A control may be applicable but planned. Never infer implementation from document existence.

## Crosswalk

Map authorized ISO requirement, Annex A, and SOC 2 Security criterion identifiers to implemented control description, owner, policy, procedure, and expected evidence. Reconcile with SoA and risk treatment. One control may serve both frameworks; avoid duplicate policies.

## Evidence expectations

Define evidence source, cadence, period, owner, external location, retention, and verification status. Do not collect or pre-create evidence. Keep sensitive artifacts outside Git.

## Assurance design

Document internal-audit independence, management-review inputs/outputs, corrective-action workflow, and readiness conditions. Skill does not perform these activities or claim their completion.
