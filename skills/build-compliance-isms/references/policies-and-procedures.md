# Policy and procedure interviews

## Policy review

For each policy, confirm scope, mandatory current requirements, roles, exceptions, monitoring, violations, related procedures, and review triggers. Keep policy concise. Do not turn a desired control into current practice.

Review policies in dependency order: security direction; assets; access; data/privacy/retention; cryptography/secrets; people/remote work; physical; suppliers/cloud; development/change; operations/network; vulnerability/malware; logging/monitoring; incidents; continuity/backup/recovery.

## Procedure review

For each procedure, confirm trigger, authorized performer, prerequisites, exact current steps, approvals, source systems, failure/escalation, recovery, output, and record. A procedure must be executable by assigned role without inventing missing steps.

Do not fabricate recurring execution. A documented procedure and blank template show design only.

## Solo-founder adaptations

Describe compensating self-review, automation, external review, or delayed independent review truthfully. Do not claim segregation of duties where none exists. Preserve requirement for impartial internal audit without assuming a particular contractor or future hire.

## Operational values

Retention, recovery, review, remediation, logging, backup, and rotation values belong to user unless authoritative requirement, documented default, or verified actual configuration supplies them. Record basis and status.
